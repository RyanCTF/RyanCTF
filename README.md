---

Five years in penetration testing, ten years across technical roles. My focus is web application and API security -- the kind of work that requires you to understand how systems are designed before you look for where they fall apart. Alongside that I run external infrastructure assessments regularly, and I'm actively building depth in internal infrastructure, cloud environments, and AI attack surfaces.

I came into offensive security through a technology apprenticeship, which means I spent years on the other side of the fence before I started breaking things professionally. That background shapes how I approach engagements: the goal isn't just to find a finding, it's to understand why it exists and what it actually means for the business. I've worked in both consultancy and in-house settings, including time at MDSec, NetSPI, and Vodafone.

When the opportunity is there, I mentor junior testers. The field has a habit of making knowledge feel harder to access than it should be, and I don't see any value in that.
